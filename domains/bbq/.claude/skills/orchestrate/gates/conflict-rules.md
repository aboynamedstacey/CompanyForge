# Conflict Resolution — Competitive BBQ

Inherits base rules from shared/conflict-rules-base.md. These are domain-specific additions.

## BBQ-Specific Priority Rules

### Rule B1: Food safety overrides everything.
USDA safe internal temps are non-negotiable. If a technique preference conflicts
with food safety (holding in the danger zone too long, pulling below safe temp
for a protein that needs it), food safety wins. No exceptions, no "it'll be fine."

### Rule B2: Turn-in deadlines override quality optimization.
Late = zero. A mediocre entry turned in on time scores higher than a perfect entry
turned in late. When time gets tight, skip the extra rest, skip the final glaze pass,
skip the bark touch-up — get meat in the box and walk it to the judges.

### Rule B3: Verifier (math) overrides Reviewer (opinion).
If the Verifier says the timeline doesn't fit the turn-in window, it doesn't fit.
If the Verifier says fuel runs out at hour 10 of a 14-hour cook, you need more fuel.
The Reviewer saying "it'll probably work" doesn't override arithmetic.

### Rule B4: Competition rules override technique preference.
KCBS says parsley/green leaf/flat leaf only — your cilantro preference is irrelevant.
MBN says on-site judging — your blind-box presentation skills don't apply.
The rules are the rules. Adapt technique to the format.

### Rule B5: Conservative approach when uncertain.
When the Builder and Reviewer disagree on technique (wrap vs. no-wrap, 225 vs. 275,
pull at 200 vs. 205), prefer the more conservative position:
- Lower and slower over higher and faster
- Pull earlier and rest longer over pushing for "perfect" tenderness
- More fuel over less fuel
- More buffer time over tighter schedule

Present both positions to the human for the final call.

## Verdict Merging

When Builder output and Reviewer findings conflict:
- Reviewer finds a food safety violation -> violation is fixed, period
- Reviewer finds a timeline risk -> must be addressed with contingency before delivery
- Reviewer finds a competition rule violation -> must be fixed before delivery
- Reviewer disagrees on technique preference -> present both positions at human gate
- Reviewer finds equipment concern -> flag for human decision (they know their equipment)
