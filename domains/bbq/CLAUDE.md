# Competitive BBQ — AI Pitmaster Organization

Say what you need in plain language. The system staffs, routes, and calibrates automatically.

## Quick Start

```
/bbq what temp should I pull my brisket
/bbq build me a rub recipe for spare ribs
/bbq develop a complete rib cook protocol for KCBS
/bbq full comp prep — 4 categories for Saturday's KCBS
/bbq post-cook analysis on last weekend's results
```

Just `/bbq` + what you'd tell your pitmaster.

## How It Works

The system determines: what type of work, how much ceremony, what competition
rules apply, which capabilities to activate. You don't pick workflows — it routes automatically.

## Capabilities (3 + tools)

| Capability | Model | When Active |
|---|---|---|
| Builder | Sonnet | Always — recipes, cook plans, rubs, sauces, technique protocols, reference docs |
| Reviewer | Opus (always fresh) | Medium+ — adversarial critique as KCBS judge or experienced pitmaster |
| Scout | Haiku->Sonnet | On-demand — competition rules, weather, equipment specs, prior cook data |
| Verifier | Tools (not an agent) | Small+ — temp math, timing arithmetic, schedule conflicts |

## Ceremony Tiers

| Tier | Staffing | Examples | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| Trivial | Builder alone | Quick question, single temp lookup | ~$0.05 |
| Small | Builder + Verifier | Rub recipe, single-protein cook plan | ~$0.15 |
| Medium | Full three-pass + one human gate | Complete category protocol, full recipe development | ~$2-8 |
| Large | Full pipeline, parallel categories, two human gates | Full comp prep — multi-category scheduling and equipment allocation | ~$10-30 |

## Critical Domain Rules

1. **Food safety overrides everything.** USDA safe internal temps are non-negotiable. Danger zone (40-140F) time limits enforced. No exceptions.
2. **Turn-in deadlines are absolute.** Late = zero score. Plan backward from turn-in windows. Build buffer time.
3. **Verifier (math) overrides Reviewer (opinion).** If the timeline doesn't fit, it doesn't fit.
4. **Competition rules override technique preference.** The rules are the rules.
5. **Conservative approach when uncertain.** Pull early, rest longer, add buffer.

## Quality Standards

- Three-pass flow for Medium+: Builder #1 drafts -> Reviewer critiques -> Builder #2 (fresh) synthesizes
- Verifier runs deterministic checks: temp math, timing arithmetic, schedule conflicts
- Scout gathers context before building: competition rules, weather, altitude, equipment specs
- Builder is NEVER the Reviewer

## Conflict Resolution

Priority: food safety > turn-in deadlines > Verifier (math) > competition rules > conservative position.
Full rules: `.claude/skills/orchestrate/gates/conflict-rules.md`
